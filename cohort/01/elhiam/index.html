the dominican republic

The Dominican Republic is a country in the Caribbean, located on the island of Hispaniola in the Greater Antilles archipelago.

Climate in Dominican republic

The Dominican The Dominican Republic has a tropical rainforest climate[120] in the coastal and lowland areas. \ Some areas, such as most of the Cibao region, have a tropical savanna climate.[120] Due to its diverse topography, Dominican Republic's climate shows considerable variation over short distances and is the most varied of all the Antilles. The annual average temperature is 25 °C (77 °F). At higher elevations the temperature averages 18 °C (64.4 °F) while near sea level the average temperature is 28 °C (82.4 °F). Low temperatures of 0 °C (32 °F) are possible in the mountains while high temperatures of 40 °C (104 °F) are possible in protected valleys

the food

  • The dominican flag (la bandera)
  • El mangu
  • Sancocho

  • Pica Pollo

These dishes are part of Dominican food and culture. Tourists love to try the cuisine of different countries, and Dominican Republic has a lot to offer.

Culture

Dominican culture is a vibrant mosaic of Spanish, African, and Taino traditions, evident in its music like merengue and bachata, its rich cuisine, the important value of family and community, and joyful celebrations like Carnival. It is characterized by the warmth of its people, a strong sense of identity and pride in their heritage, and a deep connection to the island's history.

One of the most important and colorful celebrations in the Dominican Republic is the Dominican Carnival, which is celebrated mainly in February and culminates on National Independence Day, February 27, with parades, music and dazzling costumes that reflect the joy and culture of the people
