Backstory about Germany

Germany's backstory involves a long history of fragmented states that eventually unified into a modern nation-state in the 19th century under Prussian leadership. It evolved from the Holy Roman Empire into a confederation of states after Napoleon's defeat, which was then replaced by the German Empire in 1871. This unification was followed by periods of intense conflict, including two world wars and the division of the country into East and West Germany during the Cold War, before reunification in 1990.

Pros and cons in Germany

Germany's pros include a strong economy with good job prospects, a high quality of life with excellent public transportation and healthcare, and a safe environment with many cultural activities. The main cons are a significant language barrier, slow and complex bureaucracy, high taxes, and a chilly climate that can be a challenge for some.

  • Strong economy and job market: Germany has many job opportunities, especially for skilled professionals in fields like engineering and tech.
  • High quality of life: The country offers a high standard of living with good salaries, excellent healthcare, and social benefits.
  • Excellent public transportation: The public transit system is efficient, clean, and widespread, making it easy to travel without a car.
  • Safety and culture: Germany is considered a safe country with clean cities and a rich cultural scene.
  • Educational opportunities: Public universities are often free or very low-cost.
  • Work-life balance: The country generally provides a good work-life balance.
  • Language barrier: German is the official language, and while English is spoken in many areas, a lack of fluency can be a significant hurdle in daily life and work.
  • Bureaucracy: The administrative system is known for being slow and complex, which can be frustrating for newcomers.
  • Cost of living: While affordable overall, major city centers can have high housing and rental costs.
  • Climate: The long winters can be gloomy and cold, and vitamin D deficiency is common.
  • High taxes: Germany has high income and social security tax rates
  • Housing market: Finding an affordable place to live can be very difficult in certain areas, and rentals often come unfurnished.

My connection

In a good way, "Germany" can mean "the people's land" when referring to Deutschland, its native name, which comes from the Old High German word diutisc, meaning "of the people". While "Germany" is what the Romans called the area, its native name reflects a sense of unity and belonging.

My least but favorite historical leader

Adolf Hitler was an Austrian-born German politician who was the dictator of Germany during the Nazi period from 1933 until his suicide in 1945. He rose to power as the leader of the Nazi Party, becoming the chancellor in 1933 and then taking the title of Führer und Reichskanzler in 1934.

Why he was my leat favorite leader

Adolf Hitler is one of the most reviled figures in history due to his role in orchestrating the Holocaust and initiating World War II, which caused immense suffering and led to the deaths of tens of millions of people. His actions were driven by a hateful, racist, and expansionist ideology known as Nazism. The Holocaust was the state-sponsored, systematic persecution and murder of six million European Jews between 1933 and 1945. It was a core tenet of Hitler's ideology, which preached the racial superiority of the "Aryan" race and falsely accused Jews of causing Germany's problems.

Why he was my favorite

Hitler's dealings with Hugenberg and the industrialists exemplify his skill in using those who sought to use him. But his most important achievement was the establishment of a truly national party (with its voters and followers drawn from different classes and religious groups), unique in Germany at the time.
