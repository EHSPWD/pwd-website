What is Ice-cream?

Ice cream is a sweetened frozen food typically eaten as a snack or dessert. It is usually made from dairy products, such as milk and cream, and often combined with fruits or other ingredients and flavors.

How is Ice-Cream made?

Ice-cream is made by blending milk, cream, sugar, and flavorings, then pasteurizing, homogenizing, and rapidly chilling the mixture before churning it to incorporate air and form small ice crystals, and finally freezing it solid in a blast freezer for a smooth, creamy texture

What's My Favorite Ice-cream flavor?

Rocky Road is likely my favorite because of the delightful mix of textures (creamy, chewy, crunchy) and flavors (rich chocolate, sweet marshmallow, nutty almond) that create a sensory adventure in every bite, a contrast that many find irresistibly satisfying and nostalgic, symbolizing life's "rocky" but sweet journey.
